Containment Levels

Biosafety Level Classifications

1

BSL-1 (Basic)

Suitable for work with well-characterized agents not known to cause disease in healthy adults.

  • Standard microbiological practices
  • No special containment required
  • Basic PPE (lab coats, gloves)
  • Open bench work allowed
2

BSL-2 (Moderate)

Appropriate for work with agents of moderate potential hazard to personnel and environment.

  • Biological safety cabinets
  • Autoclave access required
  • Enhanced PPE protocols
  • Restricted laboratory access
3

BSL-3 (High)

For work with indigenous or exotic agents that may cause serious or potentially lethal disease.

  • Controlled laboratory access
  • Directional airflow systems
  • Double-door entry airlocks
  • HEPA-filtered exhaust air
4

BSL-4 (Maximum)

Required for work with dangerous and exotic agents that pose high risk of life-threatening disease.

  • Class III biosafety cabinets
  • Positive pressure suits
  • Decontamination showers
  • HEPA filtration on all vents
Guidelines

Safety Protocols & Procedures

BSL-1

Standard Hygiene

Basic cleanliness protocols

  • Hand washing before and after lab work
  • No eating, drinking, or applying cosmetics
  • Proper disposal of sharps in designated containers
  • Daily decontamination of work surfaces
BSL-2

PPE Requirements

Personal protective equipment

  • Lab coats with wrist coverage required
  • Safety glasses or face shields
  • Double gloves for infectious materials
  • Closed-toe shoes with full coverage
BSL-3

Airflow Management

Ventilation and containment

  • Negative pressure room maintenance
  • Minimum 12 air changes per hour
  • HEPA filtration on all exhaust
  • Continuous air pressure monitoring
BSL-2

Decontamination

Sterilization procedures

  • Autoclave validation weekly
  • Chemical disinfection protocols
  • UV sterilization cycles
  • Waste segregation and labeling
BSL-3

Access Control

Entry and exit procedures

  • Key card access only
  • Emergency contact posted at entry
  • Mandatory safety training records
  • Logbook for all personnel entry
BSL-1

Emergency Response

Incident management

  • Eyewash stations within 10 seconds
  • Spill kits readily accessible
  • Emergency shower availability
  • First aid trained personnel on site
